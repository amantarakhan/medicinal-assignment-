L-DOPA — Active Isomer

The -NH₂ amino group is perfectly positioned to bind the PLP cofactor inside the enzyme's chiral pocket.

This 3D fit lets DOPA Decarboxylase convert L-DOPA → Dopamine, which crosses the blood-brain barrier to treat Parkinson's disease.

D-DOPA — Inactive Isomer

The flipped chiral center (C*) points the -NH₂ group the wrong way, causing steric clashes with the active site walls.

The enzyme physically cannot bind D-DOPA. This is why stereochemistry is critical in drug design.
