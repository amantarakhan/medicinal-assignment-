L-DOPA: Active Isomer

The -NH₂ amino group is correctly positioned to bind the PLP cofactor inside the enzyme's chiral pocket.

This perfect 3D fit allows DOPA Decarboxylase to convert L-DOPA → Dopamine, which crosses the blood-brain barrier to treat Parkinson's disease.

D-DOPA: Inactive Isomer

The flipped chiral center (C*) means the -NH₂ group points the wrong way, causing steric clashes with the enzyme's active site walls.

The enzyme physically cannot bind D-DOPA — this is why stereochemistry is critical in drug design.
